I am here to say I am the first college-educated person in my family for generations, and that’s because I’ve had the opportunity to become so and I took it. This means everything to me – privilege, opportunity, pride, strength, but also hardship, loneliness, confusion, and obstacles. But with the support of my friends, mentors, faculty, and staff, my journey of being a first-generation college student at the University of Michigan was truly phenomenal. I discovered my passion of serving low-income communities through the different positions I’ve worked. I grew my leadership potential by joining and then later leading student organizations. I have participated in and organized teach-ins, workshops, dialogues, full conferences, and executed both short and day-long events. And I also love memes!!
